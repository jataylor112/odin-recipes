Lasagne alla Bolognese

A traditional dish originating from the region of Bologna, Italy. It uses layers of pasta, béchamel, and ragù combined with pork and beef mince.

Ingredients

    Lasagne

  • Lasagne Pasta: 15 sheets
  • Parmesan, grated: 100 g
  • Basil, to garnish
  • Granulated Sugar: 15 g

    • Ragù Alla Bolognese

  • Pork Mince: 500 g
  • Beef Mince: 500 g
  • Pancetta, cubed: 100 g
  • Onion, finely diced: 1
  • Garlic Cloves, sliced: 2
  • Sticks of Celery, finely diced: 2
  • Large Carrot, peeled and finely diced: 1
  • Red or White Wine: 200 mL
  • Tin of Chopped Tomatoes: 1
  • Milk: 150 mL
  • Olive Oil: 3 tablespoons
  • Salt
  • Pepper

    • Béchamel

  • Butter: 50 g
  • Flour: 50 g
  • Bay Leaf: 1
  • Whole Milk, warmed: 750 mL
  • Nutmeg, grated
  • White Wine Vinegar: 1 dash
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Make the ragù. Heat 2 tbsp of oil in a heavy-based pan and brown the mince (make in batches to avoid overcrowding the pain). Once nicely browned, remove from the pan. Depending on the quality of the mince, there may be a lot of fat – if so, drain then set aside the cooked mince.
  2. Give the pan a quick wipe, then sauté the pancetta in 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  3. Add the onions, garlic, celery and carrot and gently sauté for about 5 minutes, but don’t allow them to color.
  4. Add the mince back to the pan, give everything a stir and add the wine. Turn up the heat and reduce the liquid by half.
  5. Next add the tinned tomatoes, followed by the milk. Turn down to a low heat, stir well and put a lid on the pan.
  6. Leave to simmer for 2–3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  7. To make the béchamel, melt the butter in a pan. Add the flour, cook out for a couple of minutes, then add the bay leaf. Slowly whisk in the milk and simmer over a low heat for 8 minutes, constantly stirring to avoid it catching.
  8. Grate in some nutmeg then season with salt and a dash of white wine vinegar to taste.
  9. Remove from the heat and lay a sheet of cling film over the surface of the sauce until ready to use to prevent a skin from forming.
  10. Once you have your ragù and béchamel ready (both of which can be made a day in advance if needed), it’s time to start layering up the lasagne.
  11. Preheat the oven to 180°C or gas mark 4.
  12. Begin with a layer of ragù, then a layer of béchamel, a sprinkling of Parmesan then neatly tiled lasagna sheets. The final layers should be lasagne topped with béchamel and a grating of Parmesan.
  13. Bake in the oven for 45 minutes, covering the top with foil if it's browning too quickly. Remove from the oven and serve.
