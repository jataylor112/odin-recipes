Yorkshire Pudding

A British side dish often used for traditional Sunday roast! It's a baked pudding made from eggs, floud, and milk that can be filled with bangers and mash.

Ingredients

  • Plain Flour: 4 oz. (110g)
  • Egg, large: 1
  • Milk: 1/2 British pint (10 fl oz. or 284 mL)
  • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Roast Meat or Sunflower Oil Drippings

Steps

  1. Sift the flour into a bowl and add the salt.
  2. Mix the egg into the milk, then add this mix gradually to the flour, beating with a balloon whisk until all the milk is added and the mixture is well beaten. For best results refrigerate the batter for 1 hour.
  3. Place a little of the dripping or oil into each division of a bun or large muffin tin and place the tin in the oven to heat (usually the roast joint will still be in the oven), but if cooking separately heat the oven to 200-220Â°C or Gas mark 7.
  4. Once the tin is smoking hot, fill each division with the batter and return to the oven. Remove and serve when risen, firm and brown.

    5. Note: If your egg is small, use two eggs and less milk; otherwise, the pudding will not rise.
