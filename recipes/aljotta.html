Aljotta

A popular fish dish in Malta during Lent where majority of the population adheres to Catholicism. The soup contains fish, usually rock fish, as well as lemons, white rice, tomatoes.

Ingredients

  • Small Fish (traditionally rock fish or substitute with halibut, flounder, and snapper), cleaned and cut into 4 pieces
  • Water: 8 cups (and a fish stock cube if available)
  • Pepper
  • Salt
  • Onion, chopped: 1
  • Bay Leaf: 1
  • Cloves of Garlic, chopped: 3
  • Mint, fresh if available
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Lemons: 2 (one for juice, one for decoration)
  • White Rice: 100
  • Tomatoes: 6

Steps

  1. Fry the choped onion and garlic and fry in oil until soft and golden. Slice the tomatoes and add them together with the herbs and enough water (with or without fish stock cube) to make the soup. Bring the mixture to boil and then add the fish. Cook slowly until the fish meat is white and soft.
  2. Strain the remaining mixture without throwing away the strained liquid. Put it on the stove and use it to cook the rice. Bring to boil and cook until the rice is soft to your taste. Put the fish pieces back in the soup.
  3. Add a squeeze of lemon and serve hot with lemon wedges and parsley as garnish.
