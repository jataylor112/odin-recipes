Borscht

A red beet-based soup originating from Ukraine that has since become popular across Eastern Europe. It can be served hot or cold, though this recipe calls for it to be hot.

Ingredients

  • Water: 6 cups
  • Salt: 3/4 tablespoon
  • Carrots, finely chopped: 1/2 cup
  • Green Bell Pepper, chopped and divided: 1/4 cup
  • Stalk Celery, chopped: 1/2
  • Medium Beet: 1
  • Tomatoes, canned, peeled and diced: 1/2 cup
  • Potatoes, quartered: 3
  • Butter: 1/3 cup
  • Onion, chopped: 1/2 cup
  • Canned Tomatoes: 1 1/2 cup
  • Cabbage, divided and finely shredded: 3 cups
  • Heavy Cream: 1/4 cup
  • Potatoes, diced: 3/4 cup
  • Dried Dill Weed: 1 tablespoon
  • Ground Black Pepper, to taste: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Salt and Ground Black Pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Place water, salt, carrots, 1/2 of the bell pepper, celery, beet, tomatoes, and quartered potatoes in a large stock pot over high heat. Bring to a boil.
  2. Melt 1/3 cup butter in a separate skillet over medium heat. Saute onions in butter until tender, approximately 5 minutes. Stir in tomatoes, reduce heat to medium low, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Remove 1/2 cup of sauce from skillet, and set aside. Stir half of the cabbage into the skillet with remaining sauce, and continue simmering 5 minutes more, or until tender.
  4. Remove beet from boiling liquid and discard. Remove potatoes with a slotted spoon or tongs, and place in a bowl with remaining 1 tablespoon of butter and the cream. Mash together until smooth.
  5. Return the 1/2 cup of reserved onion-tomato sauce to the stock pot. Stir in diced potatoes, and simmer until just tender but still firm, approximately 5 minutes.
  6. Increase heat to a low boil, and stir in remaining cabbage, tomato sauce, and mashed potatoes. Reduce heat and simmer a few minutes more. Stir in remaining bell pepper, season with black pepper, and serve.
