St. Honoré Cake

This dessert was first crafted in 1847 by a Parisian backery in honor of the French patron saint, Saint Honoré. It's a complex cake that uses a combination of choux pastry, puff pastry, Chiboust cream, and caramelized sugar. Although not the typical French food known to tourists, it's a delicious dessert enjoyed by many Frenchmen!

Ingredients

    Base

  • All-Purpose Flour: 125 g
  • Egg Yolk: 1
  • Butter, Softened: 50 g
  • Granulated Sugar: 15 g
  • Water: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt: 1 pinch

    • Choux Paste

  • Water: 250 mL
  • Butter: 50 g
  • Caster Sugar: 15 g
  • Plain Flour: 125g
  • Eggs: 4

    • Chiboust Cream

  • Gelatin: 15 g
  • Cold Water: 5 tablespoons
  • Milk: 1 liter
  • Vanilla Pod: 1
  • Egg Yolks: 6
  • Caster Sugar: 200 g
  • Cornstarch: 75 g
  • Egg Whites: 4
  • Icing Sugar
  • Egg, beaten, for coating: 1

Steps

  1. Prepare the base of the cake by combining all the ingredients into a smooth dough. Keep the dough in the refrigerator and continue with the making of choux paste.
  2. In a pan, heat water, butter, caster sugar, and a pinch of salt until boiling at which point immediately incorporate the flour, stir, and remove from heat.
  3. When the dough is formed into a ball, leave to cool and with a beater incorporate one by one four beaten eggs.
  4. On a buttered baking tray place the rolled out dough for the base, a circle 20 cm in diameter and 3 mm thick. Prick the dough base with a fork and with a beaten egg brush the edge.
  5. Transfer a third of the choux paste into a piping bag with a smooth tip the width of a finger, then pipe a border 3 mm from the edge of the base. Brush the border with a beaten egg.
  6. With the remaining choux paste using a piping bag and on a buttered tray pipe 20 choux balls the size of walnuts. Bake the base and the choux balls in an oven preheated at 200°C for approximately 25 minutes. When done, leave to completely cool.
  7. For the caramel sauce cook the sugar and water until they reach 145 °C. When still hot dip the choux balls into the caramel, stick them caramel side down on the border making sure they touch each other.
  8. The last is the Chiboust cream filling. In a separate bowl soften the gelatin in 5 tablespoons of cold water. Beat egg yolks with caster sugar until white and thick and add the cornstarch.
  9. Boil milk together with a vanilla pod, remove the pod and pour the milk over the egg yolk mixture. Return everything to a pan, bring to a boil while all the time whisking.
  10. Incorporate the softened gelatin until completely dissolved. Bring the cream once more to a boil and pour over the egg whites that you’ve previously stiffly whisked. Fold in the whites and cream with a metal spoon and leave to cool until on the point of setting.
  11. Fill the center of the cake with cream, sprinkle with icing sugar and broil shortly until golden. Until ready to serve, keep in a cool place.
